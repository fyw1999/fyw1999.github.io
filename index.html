Computer Vision · Visual AI Agents

Building visual systems that can perceive, reason, and act in complex real-world scenes.

I am a Ph.D. student in Computer Technology at Sun Yat-sen University (SYSU), advised by Prof. Andy J. Ma. Previously, I received my M.Eng. from Northwestern Polytechnical University and my B.Eng. from Inner Mongolia University, where I ranked 1st among 118 students.

My research spans dense object counting and tracking and trainable visual AI agents. I am particularly interested in connecting open-world visual perception with multi-turn reasoning and physical-world interaction.

Research

A

Dense Crowd Counting & Tracking

Robust perception of dense, small objects under camera motion, scale variation, and complex real-world conditions. This line of work includes MovingDroneCrowd++, video counting methods, and a tracking method for moving-drone footage.

47.4% lower counting error · 64.6% better tracking performance

B

Trainable Visual AI Agents

Learning generalizable policies that coordinate heterogeneous visual experts across multiple turns. VisHarness separates high-level perception, reasoning, and decisions from low-level visual task execution.

Generalized visual reasoning with a small amount of training data
